Features What makes OpenComm different?

Sound spatialization

Side Chats

Once you add users into a conference, there may be situations where you want to hear some people better than others. With sound spatialization, you can manipulate how you hear each participant by moving their icons around the conference. You can experience the conference call as if you were in your meeting room.

Side chats are separate conversations with people in the conference, where you can plan ahead for the conference or talk about private or sensitive subjects. You can easily go in by simply selecting the side chat and then go out by selecting another side chat or the main conference icon.

Quick Setup

Getting started with OpenComm is easy. Once installed on your phone, the app synchronizes with the contacts already available on your phone.

About Us Who's behind OpenComm?

The Team

Founded by Cornell Professor Graeme Bailey and Makoto Bentz '10, M.Eng. '11, our research team aims to add exciting functions to the status quo in multi-user communications and to implement front-end changes to reflect these changes.

We've focused on VoIP (Voice over IP), which has revolutionized user-to-user communications by allowing real-time voice communication. On the front-end, most companies have resigned themselves to simply re-implementing a phone, giving the capabilities dial a contact, voicemail, and call forwarding. While these technologies made sense to develop in the physical world, with software one can implement new solutions at little to no fixed costs to users.

We chose to focus our development work upon currently existing backend technologies such as XML and NAT and build a better software front end with them.


Professor Graeme Bailey Advisor

Professor Bailey was born and raised in England, and studied, taught and researched both in England and in France before coming to the U.S. He has been at Cornell since 1986 and is professionally interested in life in 2, 3 and 4 dimensions.

Awards

OpenComm is an award-winning team with a great group dynamic and support from several faculty members. Our work on sound spatialization is a particular point of interest and is one of the aspects that make OpenComm a unique tool for audio conferences.

OpenComm has won several awards at BOOM, an annual showcase of student research in digital technology at Cornell University. In 2011, OpenComm won the Cisco Pioneer Award and the GE Imagination in IT Award. In 2012, OpenComm won the Cisco Innovation Award, Bank of America Merill Lynch Technology Award, and Goldman Sachs Award.
